You need:

  1. Canadian citizens:
    • Valid Canadian passport or
    • Another identification document
  2. Permanent residents of Canada:
    • Valid passport or travel document and
    • Valid permanent resident card (PR card)

  1. Canadian citizens: Valid Canadian passport or identification document

    When you need it

    At the Canadian border (port of entry)

    Do you need anything else

    No, as a Canadian citizen, you don’t need a visa or permit to enter, study or work in Canada.

    Canadian passportsOpens in a new tab or Identification documents the CBSA acceptsOpens in a new tab

  2. Permanent residents of Canada: Valid passport or travel document and valid PR card

    When you need them

    At the Canadian border (port of entry)

    Do you need anything else

    No, as a permanent resident of Canada, you don’t need a visa or permit to enter, study or work in Canada.

    If you’re travelling by private vehicle (one you own, rent or borrow)

    There may be other documents you can use at the port of entry. Read the list of identification documents the CBSA accepts Opens in a new tab.

    Permanent resident cardsOpens in a new tab

    If you don’t have a PR card and you’re outside of Canada

    If your plans change and you travel to Canada by air, you must apply for a permanent resident travel document (PRTD). You will need this document to board a flight. You can apply for a new PR card once you’re back in Canada.
