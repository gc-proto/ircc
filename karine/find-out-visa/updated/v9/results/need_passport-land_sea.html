You must have a valid passport or travel document

What you need:
A valid passport or travel document
Why you need it:
A passport is the only universally accepted identification document when you're travelling
When you need it:
At the Canadian border (port of entry)
Do you need anything else:
Yes, depending on what you're planning to do in Canada, you may also need:
  • a visitor visa or super visa
  • a work permit or study permit
Your next step:
Get a passport or travel document and use this tool again to confirm what you need
There is one exception to this rule:
US citizens don't need a passport to enter Canada by car, train, bus or boat (including cruise ship). They can use one of these identification documents instead:
  • birth certificate
  • certificate of citizenship or naturalization
  • certificate of Indian status (and photo identification)

Read the list of identification documents the CBSA accepts

Reasons you might not be allowed in Canada (including past convictions)

Some people aren't allowed to come to Canada. You could be inadmissible for reasons like:

  • convictions (including driving while impaired)
  • security
  • medical conditions
