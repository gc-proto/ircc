You can use an electronic travel authorization or an existing visitor visa to return to Canada

If you don’t have a valid visitor visa

What you need:

Electronic travel authorization (eTA)

We approve most eTAs by email within minutes, but some applications can take several days if you need to submit supporting documents.

Do you need to apply for one:
Only if one of these situations apply:
  • Your existing eTA expired and you plan to leave and return to Canada
  • You don’t already have an eTA and you don’t need to extend your work permit
Valid for:
5 years or until your passport expires
Cost:
CAN $7
When you need it:
To board your flight to Canada (you don’t need it for flights within Canada or to leave Canada)
When you return to Canada, you also need:
  • Your valid work permit
  • The valid passport or travel document linked to your eTA
If your work permit expires soon:

Extend it before you leave Canada

We’ll issue you an eTA at the same time, if you don’t already have one. You can use this eTA to travel to Canada by air.

If you travel to Canada by car, train, bus or boat instead:
You will need a valid visitor visa Opens in a new tab , not an eTA

If you already have a valid visitor visa

What you need:
Visitor visa
When you need it:
To board your flight to Canada (you don’t need it for flights within Canada or to leave Canada)
When you return to Canada, you also need:
  • Your valid work permit
  • Your valid passport or travel document
If your valid visa is in an expired passport or travel document:
Your visa is still valid, so bring it with you
If your work permit expires soon:
Extend it before you leave Canada
There is one exception to this rule:
You don’t need a valid visitor visa if your work permit is valid and you return directly to Canada from a visit to the United States or St. Pierre and Miquelon.
