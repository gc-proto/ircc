How to check your application status

You can check the status of your application in the Application status tracker(opens in a new tab) .

You must create a new account if

  • this is your first time checking your application status in the application status tracker
  • you’ve been using the Client Application Status (CAS) tool(opens in a new tab)  to check your application status
    • You can’t use your CAS username and password.

To register, you need your

  •  unique client identifier (UCI) or client ID (opens in a new tab) 
  •  application number
  •  first and last name
  •  date of birth
  •  place of birth

If you’re a representative or designated person, you also need your

  •  party ID(opens in a new tab) 
  •  first and last name

Status updates will only show up after you get an acknowledgement of receipt (AOR) letter or email.

Until then, you may be able to see

  • the date we got your application
  • the name of the sponsor
  • your contact information

If you applied on paper, you can

  • create an online account
  • link your paper application to it

You’ll see any updates to your application status there.

Top questions

When can I check my application status?

You can check the status of your application after you receive an acknowledgement of receipt (AOR) letter or email from us. This confirms we received your application and that it's complete.

Before we send you an AOR or letter with your application number, we:

  1. receive your application and add it to our processing queue
  2. open it and check if it’s complete
  3. start processing it, if it’s complete

This process can take a while. We’ll return your application if:

  • it’s incomplete
  • you didn’t pay your fees
  • we receive it after a program pauses, closes or reaches its application limit

You won’t be able to check the status of your application if so.

How can I check if my application has been received?

The way to check if we received your application depends on how you applied.

Applying online

You’ll see a confirmation page after you submit your application. You’ll also get an email:

  • telling you to check your account for a confirmation message and number, or
  • confirming you submitted your application

Keep your confirmation information for your records. It can take a few hours for you to receive an email from us after you apply.

Applying by mail

Use a postal or courier service with a tracking number to ensure you get proof that your application was delivered.

Applying at a visa application centre (VAC)

VACs offer tracking services that tell you when your application has been received at our offices.

How can I update or ask about my application after it’s been submitted?

If we need more information to process your application, we’ll contact you by mail, email or through your account.

If you have a question about our request, you can:

  • use our web form
  • ask your question through your online account, if you have one.
    • Upload a document with your question in place of the document we asked for. Make sure you keep your contact information updated. That way, we can contact you if we need to.

Update your application

If you do need to change your contact information, keep checking your old contact information until we update your application.

Don’t send us changes for your contact or application information by mail

If you do, we won’t acknowledge your request or update your application. This could delay your application.

Change your mailing address

There are different ways of changing your address depending on your type of application and where you applied from.

Find out how to change your address.

Change other information

Use our web form to tell us about changes to your situation, even if you applied online.

Examples of changes include:

  • marriage or divorce
  • birth or adoption of a child
  • death of an applicant or dependant
  • getting a new passport
    • You must submit a scanned copy of your new passport.
  • changes that could affect your eligibility for the program you’re applying for, including changes in your
    • job situation
    • education
    • language skills
  • contact information updates, including
    • email
    • phone number
