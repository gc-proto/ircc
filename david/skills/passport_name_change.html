Changing the name on your passport or other travel document

Changes in your life can mean a change in your name. Find out how to get a new passport or travel document when your name changes.

Changing your passport name can cause travel problems

If the name on your passport doesn't match your travel bookings or other ID, you could face delays at border crossings, be denied boarding, or be refused entry to other countries. Make sure all travel bookings match the name on your passport.

Find the instructions for your situation

Answer a few questions to see only the steps and documents that apply to you.

Who are you applying for?

  • An adult (yourself or another adult)

    Why is the name changing?

    • Marriage, civil partnership or divorce
    • Legal name change, court order or adoption
    • Reclaiming an Indigenous name

      What document do you need updated?

      • A passport
      • A travel document
    • Dropping or rearranging given names
  • A child (under 16)

    Why is the name changing?

    • Legal name change, court order or adoption
    • Reclaiming an Indigenous name

      What document do you need updated?

      • A passport
      • A travel document

Updating your last name after marriage, divorce or civil partnership

You need to apply for a new passport. The process is the same as a regular adult passport application, but you must include extra documents.

Steps to follow

  1. Complete a new adult passport application:
    • apply for a new passport in Canada
    • apply for a new passport from the US
    • apply for a new passport from outside Canada and the US
  2. Pay the regular passport fees
  3. Include the extra documents listed below with your application

Extra documents you need

  • Supporting ID that shows your new last name
  • Your current passport, if it's still valid
  • Your proof of citizenship (it can have your old name or your new name)
  • 1 or more of the following documents (originals or copies):
    • marriage certificate
    • common-law relationship certificate
    • court order (such as a separation agreement, divorce order or judgment)
    • certificate to dissolve a registered common-law relationship
    • resumption of surname certificate

You no longer need an updated proof of citizenship

Your proof of citizenship can have your old name or your new name. Ignore the instructions in section I on the application form that say you need an updated proof of citizenship. We will update the form soon.

Requirements for your name-change documents

  • they must show the last name you want on your new passport
  • they must be in English or French (learn more about translating documents for passport applications)
  • they don't need to be signed by your guarantor

Start your passport application

Legal name changes, court orders or adoption orders

You need to apply for a new passport if your name (or your child's name) has changed due to a legal name change, court order or adoption order.

Steps to follow

  1. Complete the appropriate application:
    • apply for a new adult passport in Canada
    • apply for a new child passport in Canada
  2. Pay the regular passport fees
  3. Include the extra documents listed below with your application

Extra documents you need

  • Supporting ID that shows the new given name(s) and last name
  • Your (or your child's) current passport, if it's still valid
  • Your (or your child's) proof of citizenship (it can have the old name or new name)
  • 1 of the following documents:
    • a legal name change certificate
    • an adoption order
    • a court order

Whichever document you provide, it must show both your new name and your old name. It can be the original or a photocopy.

If you're applying by mail, we suggest sending photocopies so you can hold on to your originals.

You no longer need an updated proof of citizenship

Your proof of citizenship can have the old name or the new name. Ignore the instructions on the application form that say you need an updated proof of citizenship. We will update the form soon.

We may need further documents if they were issued outside of Canada. Documents not in English or French must be translated by a certified translator. Learn more about translations.

Changing your passport name back to a previous one

You need to submit a new application and include the documents that support the change, such as a relationship status change document, legal name change, court order or adoption order. They can be originals or photocopies.

Start your passport application

Reclaim your Indigenous name on your adult passport

You can change the name on your passport to reclaim your Indigenous name. There is no cost for this until May 30, 2026. Your updated passport will keep the same expiry date as your current one.

Names must use Roman alphabet letters

To make sure your passport is machine-readable, names can only be written in letters from the Roman alphabet and some accented letters. Most languages have alternative spellings to account for this.

Eligibility for the no-cost process

To apply at no cost, your passport must not:

  • be valid for less than 6 months
  • be damaged
  • have been declared lost or stolen

If your passport is any of the above, you must apply for a new passport and pay the fees.

Documents you need

  • Your current passport
  • 1 piece of supporting ID that shows the name change
  • A completed form PPTC 657E (PDF, 1.26 MB)
  • 2 new photos taken within the last 6 months (we'll refund the cost if you give us the receipt)
  • One of the following:
    • an updated birth certificate or citizenship certificate
    • a birth certificate or citizenship certificate with your old name and a legal name change document, court order, or adoption order (it must show both your previous name and your reclaimed name)

How to submit your application

In person

You can book an appointment online or go to a Service Canada Centre or passport office without an appointment.

Someone else can submit your application for you if you can't go yourself. Learn more about who can submit on your behalf.

By mail

Use a certified courier or traceable mail service to reduce delivery time and protect your documents.

Reclaim your Indigenous name on your travel document

You can change the name on your travel document to reclaim your Indigenous name.

Names must use Roman alphabet letters

To make sure your travel document is machine-readable, names can only be written in letters from the Roman alphabet and some accented letters.

Documents you need

  • Your current travel document
  • A photocopy of your proof of immigration status in Canada with your reclaimed name
  • 1 piece of supporting ID that shows the name change
  • A completed form PPTC 657E (PDF, 1.26 MB)
  • 2 new photos taken within the last 6 months (we'll refund the cost if you give us the receipt)

How to submit your application

Mail your form and supporting documents. Your travel document and any original documentation will be returned to you by mail. They may arrive in 2 separate packages.

Dropping or inverting given (first and middle) names

If you have multiple given names on your proof of Canadian citizenship, you can ask for your given name(s) to be slightly different on your passport. You must be applying for a new adult passport to do this.

You can:

  • drop given names
  • invert (rearrange) given names

The name you want on your passport must match the name on your supporting ID.

Start your passport application

Characters and accent marks in passports

We can only print the Roman alphabet and some accented letters in passports and travel documents. Most languages have alternative spellings of names to account for this.

Why this limitation exists

The International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO) has guidelines to make sure all passports and travel documents are machine-readable. These guidelines are followed by computer systems used by domestic and foreign border control agencies and airlines for ticket purchasing, reservations and boarding cards. This makes sure your passport or travel document works across computer systems no matter where you travel.
