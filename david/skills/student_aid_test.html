How to apply

You must apply online for a study permit, whether you are outside or inside Canada. In most cases, you should apply before you come to Canada.

Get your documents ready

When you apply, submit all of the following:

  • a letter of acceptance from a designated learning institution (DLI)
  • a provincial attestation letter (PAL) or territorial attestation letter (TAL)
  • all other documents needed for a complete application

Upload your PAL or TAL into the Provincial attestation letter (required) field in your application. If this field is missing, upload it in the Client information field. If you meet a PAL/TAL exception, provide your evidence in the Client information field.

We cannot finalize your study permit until you submit a complete application. Waiting to submit documents will cause processing delays.

Steps to apply online

Follow these steps to apply for your study permit online:

  1. Make sure you have what you need
    • a scanner or camera to create electronic copies of your documents
    • a valid credit or debit card

    If you are applying from outside Canada, check your local visa office instructions for any country-specific requirements.

  2. Read the instruction guide

    Even if you apply online, read the instruction guide before you start. It explains how to complete each field on the form.

  3. Answer the online questions

    We use your answers to create a personalized document checklist for you.

  4. Pay your fees

    In most cases, you pay processing fees and biometrics fees. We ask you to pay at the end of your application.

  5. Create your account and submit

    Sign in to your IRCC secure account or create a new one. You can use your account to pay fees, submit your application, and check the status.

    Continue to IRCC secure account

If you need an eTA or visitor visa

If you need an electronic travel authorization (eTA) or visitor visa, we issue it automatically when we approve your study permit. You do not need to apply separately or pay another fee.

Get faster processing as a doctoral student

If you are a doctoral (PhD) student, you and your accompanying family members may be able to get faster processing. Check if you are eligible for faster doctoral processing.

Fees

Application fees

In most cases, your fees include:

  • processing fees for you and anyone included on your application
  • biometrics fees

We ask you to pay at the end of your application.

Third-party fees

Depending on your situation, you may also need to pay third parties for:

  • medical exams
  • police certificates
  • language testing
  • services at a visa application centre, if you use one

You pay third-party fees directly to the provider, not through your online account.

Accepted payment methods

We accept the following payment methods:

  • credit cards and prepaid cards: Visa, MasterCard, American Express, JCB
  • Visa Debit cards
  • debit cards from a Canadian bank using Interac Online, registered for online banking

The card you use does not need to be in your name. The cardholder's name appears on the receipt but does not need to match your application.

If you use a prepaid credit card, keep it for at least 18 months after you pay. This makes refunds easier.

Biometrics

We collect your fingerprints and photo (biometrics) for most applications. If you are between 14 and 79 years old, you probably need to give biometrics.

You only need to give biometrics once every 10 years.

Biometrics fees

  • individual applicant: CAD $85
  • families applying at the same time: maximum CAD $170
  • groups of 3 or more performing artists and their staff applying for work permits at the same time: maximum CAD $255

Pay the biometrics fee when you submit your application. If you do not, you may experience delays.

What happens after you apply

  1. You submit your application and pay fees.
  2. We send you a letter telling you to give biometrics. You have 30 days from the date on the letter.
  3. You go to a collection point to give your fingerprints and photo.
  4. We start processing your application after we receive your biometrics.

Apply for a minor child

If you are applying for a study permit for a child under 18, there are additional requirements.

Who applies

A parent or legal guardian must apply on behalf of a minor child. The parent or guardian signs the application and is the representative.

Documents you need

  • a letter of acceptance from the school (if available)
  • proof of the parent-child relationship (for example, a birth certificate)
  • a custodianship declaration, if the child will not be accompanied by a parent or legal guardian in Canada
  • proof of financial support

Note: Some school boards do not issue a letter of acceptance until the child arrives in Canada. If you cannot get a letter of acceptance before applying, explain this in the Client information field of your application.

If you already have a study permit

If you are in Canada with a study permit and want to apply for your dependent children, you can apply from inside Canada. Select "initial study permit" when filling out the application for each child.

Apply from inside Canada

Only some people can apply for a study permit from inside Canada. You can apply from inside Canada if you are currently in Canada and one of the following applies:

  • you have a valid study or work permit
  • your spouse, common-law partner, or parent has a valid study or work permit
  • you are a minor child in primary or secondary school
  • you are an exchange student or visiting student
  • you completed a short-term course or study program required to be accepted at a DLI
  • you or your spouse, common-law partner, or dependent child has a temporary resident permit valid for 6 months or more
  • you are being sponsored to immigrate and have already applied for permanent residence
  • you or your spouse, common-law partner, or dependent child are subject to an unenforceable removal order
  • your spouse, common-law partner, or dependent child is an athlete, media member, member of the clergy, or military personnel on duty in Canada, or is an accredited foreign representative
  • you are a refugee claimant in Canada or a family member of a refugee claimant

If none of the above applies, you must apply from outside Canada.

If your study permit has expired

If your study permit has expired and you want to continue studying, you need to restore your status as a student. On the application form, select both "initial study permit or extension of study permit" and "restoration of temporary resident status as a student."

Most people in Canada can no longer apply for a study permit at a port of entry. You must apply online for your initial study permit and for any extensions.

Apply at a port of entry

You can apply for a study permit at a Canadian port of entry only if you are a:

  • citizen of the United States
  • permanent resident of the United States
  • person lawfully admitted to the United States for permanent residence
  • resident of Greenland
  • resident of Saint-Pierre and Miquelon

If you are eligible, tell the border officer that you want a study permit when you arrive. The officer checks that you meet the requirements and, if eligible, issues the permit. If you are not eligible, the officer refuses the permit, but you may still be able to enter Canada as a visitor.

Apply on paper

You can apply on paper only if:

  • you have a disability that prevents you from applying online, or
  • you hold an identity or travel document for non-national residents, refugees, or stateless persons

If you are eligible to apply on paper, get the paper application forms and instructions.

Venezuelan passport holders

If the printed expiry date on your Venezuelan passport has passed, add 5 years to the printed date and enter that as the expiry date on your application form. Include a letter of explanation stating: "I am a Venezuelan national with a Venezuelan passport, which has been extended for 5 years."

If your passport is still expired after adding 5 years, it is considered expired. You cannot submit an application with that passport.
