Affected by a natural disaster in Canada?

You may be able to extend your permit or restore your immigration status — even if you missed a deadline because of the disaster.

6 months

You have up to 6 months from the date you were directly affected to apply. After that, these exemptions no longer apply.

Who qualifies

You may qualify if you:

  • are currently in Canada
  • were directly affected by a natural disaster in Canada
  • had valid temporary resident status on the date the disaster affected you
  • apply within 6 months of the date you were directly affected
  • provide a signed statement (attestation) explaining when and how you were affected

This policy applies to visitors, workers, and students who held valid temporary resident status at the time of the disaster.

What you can apply for

Depending on your permit type, different exemptions and applications are available.

Visitors

Apply to restore your temporary resident status.

The normal 90-day restoration deadline is waived if you apply within 6 months of being affected.

Workers

Apply to renew your work permit and restore your status.

  • Requirement to apply before expiry is waived
  • Restrictions on applying from inside Canada are waived
  • Unauthorized work restrictions are waived

Students

Apply to renew your study permit and restore your status.

  • Requirement to apply before expiry is waived
  • Restrictions on applying from inside Canada are waived
  • Unauthorized study restrictions are waived

What counts as a natural disaster

An extreme natural event qualifies under this policy if at least one of the following is true:

  • it resulted in a local state of emergency
  • it required the evacuation of people from the affected area
  • it caused damage or disruption that the community could not recover from on its own and required outside assistance

Examples: floods, wildfires, hurricanes, tornadoes, tsunamis, avalanches, landslides, storm surges, and earthquakes.

What "directly affected" means

You are considered directly affected if you live or work in an area impacted by a natural disaster, and you experienced one or more of the following:

  • injury, illness, or other health effects
  • evacuation, displacement, or relocation
  • direct damage to your livelihood or assets, including loss of identity documents or credit cards needed to manage your immigration status
  • prolonged disruption to services (such as power or telecommunications) that prevented you from managing your status

How to apply

Submit your application through the standard IRCC process for your permit type. Include an attestation with your application.

  1. Gather your documents

    Collect any supporting documents, such as a copy of your expired or expiring permit and proof of your current status.

  2. Write your attestation

    Prepare a signed statement explaining when the natural disaster affected you and how it directly impacted your ability to manage your immigration status.

  3. Submit your application

    Apply using the regular IRCC application process for your situation:

    • Extend your stay as a visitor
    • Renew your work permit
    • Renew your study permit
    • Restore your temporary resident status

Other requirements still apply

This public policy only waives the specific requirements listed above. You must still meet all other admissibility and selection requirements under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act and the Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations.
