Visas and permits: understanding the difference

When you want to come to Canada, you may need a visa, a permit, or both — depending on why you are coming and how long you plan to stay. These are two different types of documents, and understanding the difference helps you apply for the right one.

What is a visa

A visa is an official document that allows you to travel to Canada and request entry at the border. It does not guarantee entry — a border services officer makes the final decision when you arrive.

There are two types of visas for coming to Canada:

  • Visitor visa (temporary resident visa) — for short stays such as tourism, visiting family, or attending a business meeting
  • Transit visa — if you are passing through a Canadian airport on your way to another country

A visa is stamped or attached to your passport. It shows the dates during which you may travel to Canada and request entry.

Electronic travel authorization (eTA)

If you are a visa-exempt foreign national travelling to Canada by air, you need an electronic travel authorization (eTA) instead of a visitor visa. An eTA is linked electronically to your passport. Citizens of the United States do not need a visa or an eTA.

What is a permit

A permit authorizes you to do something specific while you are in Canada — such as work or study. A permit defines the conditions of your stay, including:

  • what you are allowed to do (for example, work for a specific employer or study at a specific school)
  • how long you can stay
  • any restrictions on your activities

The main permits for coming to Canada are:

  • Work permit — lets you work legally in Canada for a limited time; may be open (work for any employer) or employer-specific (closed)
  • Study permit — required if your course or program lasts more than 6 months at a designated learning institution

Unlike a visa, a permit is issued to you when you are approved to enter Canada (or before you travel, depending on where you apply). It is a separate document from your visa.

You may need both a visa and a permit

If you are not visa-exempt, you may need a visitor visa and a work or study permit to come to Canada. If you apply for a permit, you don't need to apply for visa separately

Which one do you need

The document you need depends on your nationality, your reason for coming to Canada, and how long you plan to stay.

Visiting Canada

Tourism, family visits, or short business trips (under 6 months).

You may need a visitor visa or eTA, depending on your country of citizenship. No permit is required.

Check if you need a visa

Working in Canada

Temporary employment, including international agreements and intra-company transfers.

You need a work permit. In most cases, your work permit application will also include a visitor visa or eTA — you do not need to apply for them separately.

Explore work permits

Studying in Canada

College, university, language courses longer than 6 months, or other designated programs.

You need a study permit. In most cases, your study permit application will also include a visitor visa or eTA — you do not need to apply for them separately.

Explore study permits

How to apply

Most applications for Canadian visas and permits are submitted online through the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) secure portal.

  1. Check if you need a visa or eTA

    Use this tool to find out what travel documents you need based on your country of citizenship and how you are travelling to Canada.

  2. Create or sign in to your IRCC account

    Create a secure account to submit your application and track its status online.

  3. Check processing times

    Processing times vary. Check current times before you apply so you can plan accordingly.

  4. Pay your fees

    Pay the required application fees online. Fees differ depending on the type of document you are applying for.

Get help with your application

If you need help, you can consult an authorized immigration consultant or lawyer. Only use representatives who are authorized by the Government of Canada.
