Canadian dental benefit for children under 12

The Canadian dental benefit provided interim, direct payments to help cover dental care costs for children under 12 years old who do not have access to a private dental plan. This benefit was available for two periods between October 2022 and June 2024.

Benefit periods have closed

Both benefit periods under the Dental Benefit Act are now closed. The first period ran from October 1, 2022 to June 30, 2023, and the second from July 1, 2023 to June 30, 2024. For ongoing dental coverage, visit information about the Canadian Dental Care Plan.

Check if you were eligible

Answer the following questions to find out if you would have been eligible for the Canadian dental benefit.

1. Were you the eligible parent — the person who received the Canada child benefit — for a child under 12?

You were not eligible

Only the eligible parent — the person who received the Canada child benefit for the child — could apply for this benefit.

2. Was the child under 12 years old on December 1, 2022 (first period) or July 1, 2023 (second period)?

You were not eligible

The child must have been under 12 years of age on December 1, 2022 (first period) or July 1, 2023 (second period).

3. Did the child have access to a private dental services plan or dental care insurance through employment (yours, your spouse's, or any other person's)?

You were not eligible

The child must not have been insured under a private dental services plan or had access to dental care insurance obtained through employment.

4. Was your adjusted family net income less than $90,000 in the applicable base taxation year?

You were not eligible

Your adjusted family net income must have been less than $90,000 in the base taxation year to qualify.

5. Did the child receive, or were you intending for them to receive, dental care services in Canada during a benefit period — with costs not fully covered by a government program?

You were not eligible

The child must have received, or been expected to receive, dental care services in Canada during the benefit period, with costs not fully covered by a government program.

You appear to have been eligible

Based on your answers, you would have met the eligibility conditions for the Canadian dental benefit. See the benefit amounts and how to apply sections below for more details.

Note: Both application periods are now closed.

Benefit amounts

The amount you received depended on your adjusted family net income. If you were a shared-custody parent, you received 50% of the amounts below.

No more than two dental benefits could be paid per child. For shared-custody situations, no more than four payments (at the shared-custody rate) could be made per child.

Additional dental benefit

An additional payment was available in the second period (July 1, 2023 to June 30, 2024) if:

  • you received the benefit in the first period for a child whose dental costs exceeded $650 during that period, and no other eligible parent applied in the second period, or
  • no benefit was received for the child in the first period, you were eligible in the second period, and the child's dental costs exceeded or were expected to exceed $650 during the second period.

How to apply

Applications were submitted to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) on behalf of the Minister of Health.

There were two application windows:

  • First period: December 1, 2022 to June 30, 2023 — for dental services received or planned between October 1, 2022 and June 30, 2023
  • Second period: July 1, 2023 to June 30, 2024 — for dental services received or planned between July 1, 2023 and June 30, 2024

Application periods are closed

You can no longer apply for the Canadian dental benefit under the Dental Benefit Act. Both periods have ended.

What you need to include

An application required:

  • your name and address
  • an attestation that the child received or would receive dental care services in Canada during the benefit period, and that the child did not have access to a private dental plan
  • the name, address, and telephone number of the dentist, denturist, or dental hygienist who provided or would provide the services, and the month of service
  • the name, address, and telephone number of your employer and your cohabiting spouse or common-law partner's employer (if applicable), as of the date of service or date of application
  • any other information required by the Minister

Shared custody

If you were a shared-custody parent — sharing custody roughly equally with another person — you received 50% of the standard benefit amount. Only one application per period was permitted per child, per parent with shared custody.

Repayment and misrepresentation

The Minister of Health could determine that a person was ineligible if:

  • a previous application was based on a misrepresentation or concealment of a material fact, or
  • the applicant had been found to have committed a violation in relation to a previous application and that finding had not been overturned.

It is an offence to provide false or misleading information in an application. You may be required to repay any amounts received to which you were not entitled.
