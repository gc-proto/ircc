Merchant protections when accepting card payments

The Code of Conduct for the Payment Card Industry in Canada gives you clear rights as a merchant — so you know your costs, choose your payment options, and have a path to resolve disputes.

See your rights as a merchant

  • What the Code does for you
  • Who must follow the Code
  • Your protections under the Code
  • How FCAC enforces the Code
  • Related links

What the Code does for you

The Code of Conduct for the Payment Card Industry in Canada was introduced in 2010 and last updated in 2024. All major payment card network operators — American Express Canada, Discover, Interac, Mastercard Canada, Visa Canada, and UnionPay — have agreed to it.

Know your costs

You must be fully informed of all fees so you can forecast your monthly card-acceptance costs.

Encourage lower-cost choices

You get pricing flexibility to steer customers toward payment options that cost you less.

Choose what you accept

You decide which payment card products and networks to accept at your business.

Who must follow the Code

The Code applies to the full chain of players involved in card payments in Canada.

Payment card network operators (PCNOs)

Companies like Visa, Mastercard, and Interac that set the rules for card transactions. They must embed the Code in their network rules and require all participants to comply.

Acquirers

Entities that let you accept card payments — either directly through a PCNO contract or through a sponsoring acquiring entity. They are responsible for the downstream participants they use.

Downstream participants

Any person or business that has a direct or indirect relationship with an acquirer, is covered by a PCNO's network rules, and interacts with a merchant in Canada to provide payment processing services or equipment.

Issuers — the institutions that issue payment cards to cardholders — are also participants under the Code.

Your protections under the Code

The Code gives you specific rights as a merchant. Learn what you are entitled to:

  • Clear, advance notice before fees or contract terms change
  • The ability to cancel your contract without penalty when fees increase beyond a set threshold
  • The right to accept only the card types and networks you choose
  • Transparent disclosure of interchange rates and processing fees
  • A complaints process if a network operator or acquirer is not following the Code
Read your full merchant rights

How FCAC enforces the Code

The Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC) monitors payment card network operators to make sure they are implementing the Code and meeting their obligations.

What FCAC does and does not do

FCAC does: examine complaints to identify patterns of non-compliance by payment card network operators.

FCAC does not: resolve individual disputes between merchants and operators, or provide compensation or redress to individual merchants.

If you believe a payment card network operator is not following the Code, you can file a complaint directly with the operator.
