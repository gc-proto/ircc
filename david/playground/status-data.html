What changed in the comments

The extra volume isn't more questions — it's an outage

Reading the comments before and after May 20 shows the rise isn't simply "more of the same." The kind of comment changed. Before, people mostly asked how to use the tracker or where to find their number. After, they report the tracker itself is broken — citing "503 Service Unavailable" errors and saying it's been down "for weeks." That single shift accounts for the overwhelming majority of the increase.

"Tracker is down / error" — share of status comments per week

Before May 20

Mostly how-to & access

Navigation and literacy questions: how to create an account, where to enter a receipt or UCI number, how long things take. The tool works; people just need help using it.

After May 20

Mostly "it's broken"

Failure reports, not questions. The tracker returns errors or won't load at all — repeatedly named as "503 Service Unavailable" and described as down for days or weeks.

Why this matters: a volume rise from questions would point to a content or wayfinding fix. A rise from outage reports points to a service-reliability incident that began around May 20 and was still generating complaints in mid-June. The feedback box is, in effect, acting as an outage monitor — and it has been flashing red for a month.
