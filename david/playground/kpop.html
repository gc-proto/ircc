A listening portrait: Stray Kids on Spotify

An interactive read of one fan's play history across the full Stray Kids discography — 309 tracks, 69 releases, from the 2018 debut to early 2026. It's a story of a recent, intense discovery, a clear taste for high-tempo tracks, and a listener who almost never hits skip.

Each track carries its play count, total listening time, tempo (BPM), and average share of the song actually played. Listening time is converted from milliseconds to hours. "Completion" is the average percentage of each track played before moving on.

A discovery that happened all at once

Plays grouped by each release's year, stacked by release type. The back catalogue (2018–2023) draws a steady trickle; then 2024 and 2025 erupt — the signature of someone who found the group recently and devoured everything at once.

Where the hours went

Every album sized by the time poured into it and coloured by release type. Together they account for more than a thousand hours of listening. A handful of 2024–2025 releases swallow most of the canvas.

The faster the song, the harder it's played

Each dot is a track: tempo across the bottom, plays up the side, bubble size is total listening time. The cloud tilts upward — high-tempo tracks (150–200 BPM) rack up noticeably more plays than the slow ones.

A listener who doesn't skip

Each track placed by how much of it gets played on average. Almost everything clusters hard against 100%. The rare tracks drifting left — the ones that get cut short — are the exceptions that prove the devotion.
