Affected by a natural disaster in Canada? Extend or restore your immigration status

If you are a temporary resident affected by a natural disaster in Canada, you may be able to extend your permit, restore your status, or both — even if you missed deadlines because of the disaster.

  • What counts as a natural disaster
  • What "directly affected" means
  • How to apply

Who this public policy covers

This policy applies to visitors, workers, and students who had valid temporary resident status on the date they were directly affected by a natural disaster in Canada.

Who qualifies

You may qualify if you:

  • are currently in Canada
  • were directly affected by a natural disaster in Canada
  • had valid temporary resident status on the date the disaster affected you
  • apply within 6 months of the date you were directly affected
  • provide an attestation explaining when and how you were affected

What you can apply for

Depending on your situation, different exemptions and applications are available to you.

Visitors — restore your status

If you held valid temporary resident status as a visitor, you can apply to restore your temporary resident status. The normal 90-day restoration deadline is waived if you apply within 6 months of being affected.

Workers — renew your work permit and restore your status

If you held a valid work permit, you can apply to:

  • renew your work permit (the requirement to apply before it expires is waived)
  • extend your stay or restore your temporary resident status

Restrictions on applying for a work permit from inside Canada and requirements related to unauthorized work are also waived for eligible applicants.

Students — renew your study permit and restore your status

If you held a valid study permit, you can apply to:

  • renew your study permit (the requirement to apply before it expires is waived)
  • extend your stay or restore your temporary resident status

Restrictions on applying for a study permit from inside Canada and requirements related to unauthorized study are also waived for eligible applicants.

What counts as a natural disaster

An extreme natural event qualifies as a natural disaster under this policy if at least one of the following is true:

  • it resulted in a local state of emergency
  • it required the evacuation of people from the affected area
  • it caused significant damage or disruption that the community could not recover from on its own and required assistance from other jurisdictions

Examples include floods, wildfires, hurricanes, tornadoes, tsunamis, avalanches, landslides, storm surges, and earthquakes.

What "directly affected" means

You are considered directly affected if you live or work in an area impacted by a natural disaster, and you experienced one or more of the following:

  • injury, illness, or other health effects
  • evacuation, displacement, or relocation
  • direct damage to your livelihood or assets, including loss of documents (such as identity documents or credit cards) needed to manage your immigration status
  • prolonged disruption to services (such as power or telecommunications) that prevented you from managing your status

How to apply

Submit your application through the standard IRCC process for your permit type. Include the following with your application:

  1. Gather your documents

    Collect any supporting documents for your application, such as a copy of your expired or expiring permit and proof of your current status.

  2. Write your attestation

    Prepare a signed statement (attestation) explaining when the natural disaster affected you and how it directly impacted your ability to manage your immigration status.

  3. Submit your application

    Apply using the regular IRCC application process for your situation:

    • Extend your stay as a visitor
    • Renew your work permit
    • Renew your study permit
    • Restore your temporary resident status

Apply within 6 months

You must submit your application within 6 months of the date you were directly affected by the natural disaster. Applications received after that deadline will not be eligible for these exemptions.

Other requirements still apply

This public policy only waives specific requirements listed above. You must still meet all other admissibility and selection requirements under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act and the Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations.
