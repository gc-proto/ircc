Two decades of pre-monsoon heat in Rajasthan

An interactive look at daily climate-model data for nine districts of Rajasthan, India, over the March-to-June hot season from 2006 to 2025 — 21,960 district-days in total. The headline isn't a rising average. It's that extreme-heat days are striking harder, more often, and clustering in the most recent years.

Source data: daily ERA5-style reanalysis fields (temperature, wind, humidity, soil, radiation) for 9 districts × 20 years × 122 days per year. A day is flagged as a heatwave day in the dataset's HEATWAVE field. All temperatures shown in degrees Celsius (converted from Kelvin).

The shape of the hot season, year by year

Each row is a year; each column is a day from March 1 to June 30. Colour shows the 9-district average. Switch between the average daytime high and the share of districts under heatwave conditions.

Extreme-heat days don't trend — they spike

Bars show heatwave days per district each year; the line tracks a temperature metric. The average barely moves across 20 years, but heatwave days swing wildly and the biggest spikes (2010, 2024) sit in the record's two halves unequally.

Where the heat lands

Each district is placed by its coordinates. Bubble size and colour show heatwave days. Drag the slider or press play to move through the years and watch the hotspots shift across the desert north-west and the south-east.

A flat average hiding a fatter tail

The distribution of every district-day's daytime high, split into the first decade (2006–2015) and the second (2016–2025). The averages are almost identical — but the second decade carries more of its weight in the extreme-heat tail.
